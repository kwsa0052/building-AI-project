# Smart Kitchen Sustainability Assistant

Building AI course project

## Summary
This project is an AI assistant for professional kitchens that helps businesses reduce energy use, food waste, and water consumption. The system analyzes kitchen equipment usage and gives practical suggestions for more sustainable and cost-effective operations.

## Background
Professional kitchens use a lot of electricity, water, and raw materials every day. At the same time, many restaurants, hotels, and catering businesses want to become more sustainable but may not know where to start or which actions have the biggest impact.

The motivation behind this project is to make sustainability easier to understand and apply in daily kitchen operations. Instead of only showing technical data, the AI would translate it into clear recommendations that support both environmental goals and business value.

## How is it used?
The tool would be used by restaurant managers, chefs, hotel kitchen staff, and companies that buy professional kitchen equipment. A user could enter information about kitchen operations, such as energy usage, type of equipment, working hours, and waste levels.

The AI would then provide recommendations, for example:
- which equipment uses the most energy
- how to reduce unnecessary water consumption
- how to optimize daily routines
- how to communicate sustainability benefits to business customers

The system could be used as a web platform or as part of a sales support tool for B2B companies in the kitchen equipment industry.

## Data sources and AI methods
The project could use data from:
- equipment energy consumption data
- water usage reports
- maintenance records
- food waste measurements
- sustainability reports from manufacturers
- customer usage patterns

Possible AI methods include:
- **classification** to identify high-risk areas for waste or inefficiency
- **regression** to estimate future energy or water consumption
- **recommendation systems** to suggest the most suitable sustainable actions
- **natural language processing** to turn technical sustainability data into simple customer-friendly messages

## Challenges
One challenge is that kitchens are very different from each other, so the recommendations may need to be adapted to each business. Another challenge is access to reliable and complete data, because some kitchens may not measure energy, waste, or water use accurately.

There is also a risk that the AI gives advice that sounds useful in theory but is difficult to apply in practice. For that reason, human feedback and real testing would be important.

## What next?
In the future, the project could be expanded by connecting it directly to smart kitchen equipment and sensors. That would allow real-time monitoring and more accurate recommendations.

The system could also be developed into a decision-support tool for sales teams, helping them explain sustainability value to B2B customers in a more concrete and measurable way. Another next step would be to include carbon footprint calculations for different kitchen workflows and equipment choices.
